What the picture says

AES owns the things that matter most for trusting your data: it's faster, vetted, and used everywhere. Nothing we built changes that — and we didn't try to.

Our system stretches out on the other side: modern protocol features baked in — wrong-key safety, forward secrecy, and a quantum-ready handshake — wrapped around a vetted core.

The honest bottom line

We did not beat AES, and we never could — it's faster and it's trusted by the whole world. What we actually built is a complete, modern encryption system that uses AES as its safety net and adds an experimental home-made wall on the outside, plus the protocol pieces (forward secrecy, quantum-ready handshakes, wrong-key safety) that a serious system needs.

The home-made keystream survived every attack we could throw at it — but that only means we couldn't break it. Real proof is the last step left: letting outside cryptographers try. Until then it stays clearly labelled unvetted — never the only lock on real data.
